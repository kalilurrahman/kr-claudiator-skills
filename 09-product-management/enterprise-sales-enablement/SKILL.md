# Enterprise Sales Enablement

Sales enablement is the ongoing process of equipping salespeople with the content, knowledge, and tools they need to effectively engage buyers and close deals. For enterprise products, this means deep product knowledge, competitive positioning, and materials that address the complex, multi-stakeholder buying process.

## Core Enablement Materials

```markdown
## 1. Battle Card (1 page per competitor)

**[Competitor Name] vs [Your Product]**

### When You Win
- Customer values [your strength]; competitor weak there
- Large enterprise deals (>500 seats) — competitor doesn't scale
- Customers with existing [your integration partner] — native integration

### When You Lose (and why)
- Price-sensitive SMB: competitor is cheaper for small teams
- Heavy Microsoft shop: competitor has deeper MS Office integration

### Their Pitch Against You
"[Your Product] is too complex / too expensive / too new"

### Your Response
"Complexity: Our customers achieve first value in 48 hours — here's a case study.
Cost: Our enterprise ROI calculator shows average 3.2x ROI in year 1.
New: Acme Corp (Fortune 500) deployed us 18 months ago — reference available."

### Discovery Questions to Use
"How are you currently solving [problem]?"
"Who else evaluated [competitor] — what did they find?"
"What happens if this project doesn't succeed?"

### Traps to Avoid
Never: "They're much worse than us" — sounds defensive
Never: Demo head-to-head without knowing their pain point first
